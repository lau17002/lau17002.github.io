Overview

Purpose

College life can be lots of fun, but it can also get expensive. It is necessary to have a good spending budget to get through! But do you have the time to create your budget and to track all your expenses? If you are like any other college student, the answer is no. By joining with us at AtCost, I will help you create flexible and personal budgets, with an easy expense tracker to make sure that you are keeping to your goals. From rent, groceries, pet costs, and to eating out we can create a budget that works for you. Get refocused and have the college experience that you deserve.

Audience

This site provides Budget services to college students throughout the Midwest. Students from Brigham Young University-Idaho is our main focus. Included in our focus on college students are new families that have to juggle the costs of maintaining a family and school at the same time.

Home page

Mission Statement

We want to help individuals set up a budget that will help them to save for an item that they want, or to make sure that they are not spending more than their income. Our goal is to help customers understand their main expenses and how to reduce them appropriately in the future. After their package expires, we want customers to continue to use the software provided with their new skills to continue saving!

Customer Use

Once an account is created, customers have access to an expense tracker, savings total, budget software, and consulting services. These budgets are personalized to the client and their specific income and expense types. They can either create this on their own with the aid of the Budget Software or they can have a consultant assigned to them for a more personalized experience.

Prices and Packaging

Savings Total. FREE
To view, clients need to log in and view the expense tracking feature. Each client can see a comprehensive total of all savings, starting at the date of account creation at the top of the expense tracker. It can be viewed on a yearly or monthly basis.

Expense Tracking. FREE
This is a free feature included with the creation of an account. It allows clients to track expenses and to put them in a category. Included is a comprehensive list of totals in each category to allow for easy comparison between budgeted and actual expenses.

Budget Software. $49/year ($4/month)
The software by itself can be purchased on a yearly or monthly basis. This includes access to software through the client portal and templates to start creating a personal budget.

Consulting services. $10 per meeting
The first meeting with a client will be an hour long to set up expectations and the initial budget. Each meeting after that will be for thirty minutes to review success and revise as needed. Can be held virtually or physically depending on the clients needs.

Budget Software and Consulting services. $39/month
This package includes all services tied to the budget software with weekly consulting services (thirty minutes per meeting). A $8 fee will be charged for any meetings canceled within a 48-hour period before the meeting occurs.

Budget Plan

1. Calculate Net income
Net income is the amount earned less any taxes. For a college student it can also include money received from grants, scholarships, loans, or assistance from parents. If you have a fixed wage them calculating net income for the month is simple. If you work on an hourly basis, we will need to find an average amount of income.

2. List Monthly Expenses
Common expenses for the average college student can include school supplies, rent, groceries, dining, household items, transportation, utilities, insurance, loan payments and then entertainment. This is not an all-inclusive list, during the first meeting we will discuss the expenses that the client has each month.

3. Organize expenses between fixed and variable
This will be done in the software during the initial client meeting. Fixed expenses are items that cannot be avoided or easily minimized. For example, rent, groceries, and transportation. Variable expenses are more optional such as entertainment, dining, and travel.

4. Determine monthly costs
Once all expenses have been determined, a list will be made to represent all expenses that are normal for the client and the amount that is expected for a month. For items that are more variable, an estimate will be made for the first month of the budget.

5. Adjust
For the best results, we suggest each client uses our expense tracker so that at the end of the month expected versus actual costs can be compared and adjusted. Sometimes the amount spent will need to be adjusted if the client is unhappy with the amount spent. As each month goes by, the estimates will become more accurate as more data is obtained.
